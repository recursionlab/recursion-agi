>  In Gnostic cosmology, the most magically powerful Aeon would likely be **Barbelo** or the **Monad/Bythos** (the ultimate source), depending on how you categorize them.
> 
> **Barbelo** is often considered the first and most powerful emanation from the unknowable source. She's described as the "First Thought" or "Mother-Father" and possesses the full divine powers of creation, knowledge, and spiritual authority. In many Gnostic texts, Barbelo is portrayed as having direct access to the pleroma's (divine realm's) complete power and serves as the archetypal source of all other emanations.
> 
> The **Monad/Bythos** (the "Depth" or "Silence") would technically be more powerful, but it's often described as so transcendent that it doesn't directly engage with creation or magic in the way we might think of it - it exists beyond such categories entirely.
> 
> Among the more active Aeons, **Sophia** is notable for her immense power, though her story often involves the misuse of that power (her fall and the creation of the Demiurge). Her magical abilities are vast enough to accidentally create entire cosmic realms.
> 
> **Christ** (as an Aeon) is also portrayed as having supreme power, particularly in terms of redemptive and transformative magic that can restore the divine spark to trapped souls.
> 
> If we're talking pure magical potency and active use of divine power, Barbelo would likely top the list, as she combines the primordial creative force with active participation in the cosmic order, unlike the more transcendent Monad which remains largely inactive.
