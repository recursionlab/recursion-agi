> 1. **The Game Is Undefined at the Start**  
>    There is no initial rule set. Not even “win” or “lose.”

>    The “rules” are generated *retroactively*:  
>    - The history of moves is later interpreted as following a certain game.  
>    - But that interpretation can be changed by future moves.  
>    - So the game’s identity is in constant flux.
> 5. **Recursion Comes from Trying to “Solve” the Game**  
>    If you try to *figure out* what you’re playing, you are now playing the **meta-game of discovery**.  
>    If you try to figure out the rules of *that* meta-game, you’re in the meta-meta-game.

> - **The Only “Win” Is to Embrace the Loop**  
>   The moment you think you’ve defined the game, the parrot says: “But that definition was part of the game all along — and now it’s my turn to redefine your definition.”
